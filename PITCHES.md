## 1. Margo's Logos — Modesto
**Phone:** (209) 571-3413 · **Address:** 3616 Morristown Rd  
**Mockup:** `mockups/margos-logos.html`  
**Gap:** Their listed website (`margoslogos.com`) loads an error page.

### Opener
> “Hi, is this Margo’s Logos? My name is [Name]. I build websites for Modesto shops. I was looking you up for embroidery and your website address is down — it shows an error. I mocked up a clean replacement with a quote form for teams and logos. Can I text it over, or stop by for five minutes?”

### If they say “we’re fine on Facebook”
> “Totally fair. The difference is Google — when someone searches ‘embroidery Modesto,’ a real site with your phone and a quote button usually beats a Facebook page. I can keep it simple: three services, gallery, call button.”

### Offer
- Fix/replace the dead domain (or new domain if they prefer)
- Quote request form → text/email
- Photo gallery of past hoop jobs
- Price range: suggest a fixed one-pager package; collect 50% to start

### Close
> “If you like the mock, I can have a live version up in about a week. Want Tuesday or Thursday for a quick walkthrough?”

## Universal objection handlers

| They say | You say |
|---|---|
| “Too expensive” | “I do a fixed one-page price — no monthly lock-in unless you want hosting/care. Happy to quote after you see the mock.” |
| “I’ll think about it” | “Totally. I’ll text the sample link today. If I don’t hear back, I’ll check in once next week — fair?” |
| “My nephew can do it” | “Great — if he wants a starting design, this mock is free to use as a reference. If it stalls, I’m here.” |
| “We get enough word of mouth” | “Keep word of mouth. The site just catches the strangers already searching — you’re leaving those on the table.” |

## Suggested package (say out loud)
1. One-page custom site from the mock  
2. Mobile-friendly + click-to-call  
3. Contact / quote form to your phone or email  
4. Google Business Profile basics (optional add-on)  
5. 1 round of revisions after first draft  

Fill in your real price before you go. Don’t negotiate against yourself on the first call.
